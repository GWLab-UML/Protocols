# MSX (*Haplosporidium nelsoni*)
MSX also known as Multinucleated Sphere X disease is caused by a single‐celled Protozoan parasite. The parasite spreads through oyster tissues, causing major oyster mortality. The disease is influenced by environmental conditions such as salinity and temperature, and management efforts focus on developing resistant oyster strains and limiting the spread between oyster beds.

# Dermo (*Perkinsus marinus)*

## Symptoms
- Weak or unhealthy oysters
- Transparent or retracted mantle tissue
- Tissue damage in the digestive gland, gills, mantle, and gonads
- Hemocyte infiltration and destruction of tissues in severe infections
## Diagnosis
- Histological examination of oyster tissues
- Fluid Thioglycollate Medium (RFTM) assays
- PCR-based molecular testing (not used in our lab)

# Vibrio (*Vibrio alginolyticus*) 

## Symptoms in Oysters
- Weak or dying larvae and juvenile oysters
- Tissue necrosis and damage to the mantle
- Bacterial invasion of soft tissues
- Reduced survival in hatchery systems
