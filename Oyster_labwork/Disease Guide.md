# MSX (*Haplosporidium nelsoni*)

What is it? 
	MSX also known as Multinucleated Sphere Unknown disease is caused by a single‐celled Protozoan parasite. The parasite spreads through oyster tissues, causing major oyster mortality. The disease is influenced by environmental conditions such as salinity and temperature, and management efforts focus on developing resistant oyster strains and limiting the spread between oyster beds.

Up close digestive cells with a MSX cluster of cells
Two major life stages have been identified: small parasite cells and spores (shown above) visible in oyster tissues under a microscope. 
## Transmission

The exact transmission method is unknown, but researchers believe an intermediate host may be involved. The disease does not spread directly from oyster to oyster.  What is known is the infection begins in the gill and mantle tissues before quickly spreading throughout the oyster, and spores may be released through the oyster’s feces.
## Environmental Conditions
- More severe in **high salinity** waters
- Suppressed by **low salinity** and **cold temperatures** 

# Perkinsus (*Perkinsus marinus)*

## Symptoms
- Weak or unhealthy oysters
- Transparent or retracted mantle tissue
- Tissue damage in the digestive gland, gills, mantle, and gonads
- Hemocyte infiltration and destruction of tissues in severe infections
## Transmission
The parasite is waterborne and can spread directly from oyster to oyster through infective stages released into the environment.
## Environmental Conditions
- More common in warmer temperatures above 25°C
- Infection levels may increase with higher salinity conditions
## Diagnosis
- Histological examination of oyster tissues
- Fluid Thioglycollate Medium (RFTM) assays
- PCR-based molecular testing (not used in our lab)

# Vibrio (*Vibrio alginolyticus*) 

## Symptoms in Oysters
- Weak or dying larvae and juvenile oysters
- Tissue necrosis and damage to the mantle
- Bacterial invasion of soft tissues
- Reduced survival in hatchery systems
## Transmission
Vibrio bacteria naturally occur in coastal and estuarine waters. Oysters filter seawater, allowing bacteria to accumulate in their tissues. Infection spreads through contaminated water and direct exposure.
## Environmental Conditions
- Most common in warm water temperatures
- Higher abundance in brackish and coastal waters
- Outbreaks often increase during summer months
## Human Health Risks 
Since humans can become infected by eating raw or undercooked oysters contaminated with _Vibrio_. Symptoms may include:
- Vomiting and diarrhea
- Abdominal pain
- Severe bloodstream infections in vulnerable individuals
