## I. Preparing stock culturing media
#### Day 0: autoclave necessary materials

- Two 1000 mL glass screw cap bottles (for PCM) 
- Glass vacuum filter funnel
- Glass vacuum filter base
#### Day 1: make 1L PCM

**Materials:**
- DI water
- Instant Ocean 
- DME (4° C fridge)
- Ham's F12 (4° C fridge)
- L-glutamine (RT)
- Glycine betaine or betaine (4° C fridge)
- Glucose (RT)
- Sodium bicarbonate (RT)
- Weigh paper
- Spatula
- Two 1000 mL glass screw cap bottles - *autoclaved*
- Vacuum filtration rig (funnel, base, clamp) - *autoclaved*
- 1000 mL vacuum flask
- 0.22 um filter paper
- Vacuum pump and tubing
- Tweezers

**Protocol:** 
1. Make 16 g/L saltwater with Instant Ocean by adding 16 g of Instant Ocean to 1000 mL of DI water in sterile glass screw cap bottle—swirl until fully dissolved 
2. To the 16 g/L saltwater add:
	- 4.2 g DME
	- 5.4 g Ham's F12 
	- 0.15 g L-glutamine 
	- 0.015 g Glycine betaine 
	- 0.5 g glucose
	- 0.65 g Sodium bicarbonate
3. Set up the vacuum filtration rig in the BSC; spray each item with ethanol before bringing them into the BSC
	- Vacuum filtration rig (funnel, base, clamp)
	- 1000 mL vacuum flask
	- Vacuum tubing
	- 0.22 um filter paper
	- 1000 mL saltwater mixture
	-  Second empty glass screw cap bottle 
4. Set up the vacuum pump on the table outside of the BSC
5. Connect one end of the tubing to the side arm of the flask and the other to the pump
6. Firmly insert the filter base into the filtration flask 
7. Place a 0.22 um filter paper onto the top of the filter base with tweezers
8. Carefully put the filter funnel on top of the filter base, sandwiching the filter paper, and use the clamp to secure
9. Turn on the vacuum pump
10. Filter saltwater mixture by pouring it into the filter funnel slowly, making sure not to overflow the filter funnel
11. Slowly pour filtered PCM into sterile 1000 mL glass screw cap bottle and label "Perkinsus Culturing Media" including the date and initials
12. Store sterile PCM in the 4°C fridge

**Protocol:**
1. Add RFTM, nystatin, ==chloramphenicol==, and penicillin/streptomycin into a 100 mL amber screw cap bottle, ==swirl until dissolved?== and label "abRFTM" with the date and initials
	- Use a sterile stir bar and stir plate if necessary to completely dissolve
2. For each oyster, label eight 5 mL tubes with the date, "abRFTM", and tissue type:
	- Two "M" for mantle, two "R" for rectum, two "G" for gill, and two "L" for muscle
	- If dissecting multiple oysters, also label where they came from to keep samples distinct
3. In BSC, fill each tube with ==~2mL?== abRFTM (enough for the tissue to be fully submerged) using a glass serological pipette
4. On the bench, set up a flame sterilization station (bunsen burner, 100% ethanol, empty beaker, lighter) 
5. Carefully flame sterilize empty beaker, then dissecting scissors and forceps 
6. On a tray, shuck an oyster and dissect a large piece of the mantle
7. Cut the tissue in half and put each smaller piece into the appropriately labeled tube and then replace cover
	- **Make sure the tissue is fully submerged!**
8. Repeat steps 6-7 for each remaining tissue types (rectum, gill, and muscle)
	- If using multiple oysters, repeat steps 5-8
9. Incubate tubes at 28ºC for 5-10 days
	- Use a dark incubator or tent the rack with foil as RFTM is light-sensitive

At this point, you should have two tubes of each tissue type per oyster submerged in abRFTM. One of these tubes will be used for incubating and staining with iodine to determine if the oyster is infected with Perkinsus; the other will be used for homogenizing and inoculating in abPCM.

#### Day 8: RFTM assay

Retrieve one tube of each tissue type per oyster and perform [RFTM assay](RFTM.md#III.%20RFTM%20assay). If Perkinsus is identified, proceed to abPCM inoculation with the replicate abRFTM tube of the same tissue. If no Perkinsus is identified, the replicate abRFTM tube of the same tissue can be discarded in the correct hazardous waste bottle.

- 98 mL PCM

- 2 mL of penicillin/streptomycin (aliquoted, -20ºC) 
- Bunsen burner
- Forceps  
- 100% ethanol
- Ethanol lamp 
- Empty beaker (for holding sterile tools)
- Homogenizer 150
- Homogenizer probes - *autoclaved*
	- ==how many per oyster?== 
- DI water
- Soapy water
- Replicate abRFTM tubes with confirmed infected tissue (28ºC incubator)
- 6-well non-treated culturing plates - *sterile*
	- 1 well per replicate abRFTM tube

**Protocol:**
1. Add PCM, ==nystatin==, and penicillin/streptomycin into a 100 mL glass screw cap bottle, ==swirl until dissolved?== and label "abPCM" with the date and initials
	- Use a sterile stir bar and stir plate if necessary to completely dissolve 
2. For each piece of confirmed infected tissue, label a new 5 mL tube with the tissue type
	- If dissecting multiple oysters, also label where they came from to keep samples distinct
	- These are temporary/only for homogenizing so no need to date them
3. Label an additional 5 mL tube "C" for control
4. In BSC, fill each tube with 2mL abPCM using a glass serological pipette
5. On the bench, set up a flame sterilization station (bunsen burner, 100% ethanol, empty beaker, lighter) 
6. Carefully flame sterilize empty beaker and then forceps 
7. Retrieve the replicate abRFTM tubes with confirmed infected tissue and transfer each piece of tissue into the appropriately labeled abPCM tube 
	- **Make sure the tissue is fully submerged!**
	- The remaining abRFTM can be discarded into the correct hazardous waste bottle
8. In the fume hood, set up the homogenizer, homogenizing probes, DI water, and abPCM tubes
9. Insert sterile plastic probe into Homogenizer 150, making sure it locks into place
10. Submerge probe in PCM tube, hovering just above the bottom; slide speed 3 clicks to the right, flip homogenizer on, hold for 10 seconds 
	- _Never run homogenizer dry_
11. Carefully remove the probe once the homogenizer has stopped and check if any tissue is still intact; if there is visible tissue, repeat step 10
12. Clean probe immediately as follows: run for 10s in DI bath, run for 10s in soapy water
13. Remove probe and leave in soapy water
14. Repeat steps 9-13 for all tubes
15. Once all pieces of tissue have been homogenized, move to the BSC
16. In a 6-well plate, add 2mL abPCM to each well using a glass serological pipette
17. Pipette the contents of each tube (2mL) into a prepared well; repeat until all samples have been transferred
	- Make sure to label the plate clearly! 
18. Incubate at 28ºC for 7-14 days

Finish cleaning probes by rinsing with DI water and letting air dry before wrapping in foil and autoclaving to sterilize.

#### Day 16: check for cell proliferation

Check the well plate(s) under the microscope to see if any cells are present. (==insert picture of what cells look like?==)

Proceed to serial dilution protocol—woohoo!

**If there are only a few cells:**
