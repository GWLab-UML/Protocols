# Bleach Baths
In 618 there should always be two old Instant Ocean 5-gallon buckets that are used as a 5% bleach bath and DI bath, respectively. Make sure to close the bleach bath when not in use as the bleach will actually evaporate, making it a less effective cleaning step. Additionally, the bleach bath should always be labeled with the date so that it can be replaced every 2 months. At such a low concentration this can be poured down the sink drain with the tap running to further dilute. DI water can be found in 601. 

# Carboy Cultures
Larger volumes of algae to feed out to oysters. Kept constantly bubbling and as sterile as possible since contamination with other algae species OR bacteria is of high concern. As these carboys are actively being used (i.e. algae is being taken out) they will need to be re-bloomed to prevent population crashes and replenish nutrients. 

## Starting a New Carboy
Goals:
- Start fresh with sterile materials and sterile algae
- Replace existing carboy that has been re-bloomed 3 times

Materials:
- Carboy, autoclaved
	- **Important:** only the five carboys with volumetric markings are safe to autoclave
- Large rubber stopper with three drilled holes and three hard pieces of tubing, autoclaved
- 2 modified serological pipettes, bleached
- 2 long pieces of tubing, bleached
- 1 short piece of tubing, bleached
- 2 small airline clamps, bleached
- 5 rubber connectors, bleached
- 15L DI Water
- 487g Instant Ocean (32.5g/L)
- 25mL glass serological pipette, autoclaved
- 2L liquid algae culture
- 85mL F/2 media, sterile and kept at 4ºC (5mL/1L *total volume*)
	- Store-bought F/2 media is used at different concentration (1mL/1L)

Steps:
Outside of hood—
1. Measure out Instant Ocean and add to carboy, immediately cover with tinfoil
2. Put carboy on rolling cart and fill with DI water, immediately replace tinfoil
	- Periodically swirl carboy to help dissolve salt

In the hood—
1. Spray entire hood working surface with 70% ethanol
2. Spray all materials except liquid algae culture with 70% ethanol before putting them into the hood
3. Close the hood and turn on the UV light for 5 minutes **DO NOT UV STERILIZE ALGAE**
4. Pipette F/2 media into carboy
5. Pour liquid algae culture into carboy
6. Attach serological pipettes to the bottom of the rubber stopper using rubber connectors, one is for air and one is for siphoning
	- Make sure to attach the pipettes to the longer pieces of hard tubing; the shorter piece is for the exhaust
7. Put stopper on the carboy and confirm the serological pipettes do not touch the bottom
8. Attach long pieces of tubing to the air and siphoning hard tubing coming out the top of the stopper
9. Attach a short piece of tubing to the exhaust hard tubing coming out of the top of the stopper
10. Slide on the small airline clamps to the siphoning and exhaust airlines and squeeze them shut
11. Outside of the hood, use the mallet to lightly tap down the rubber stopper to ensure a tight seal

In the oyster room—
1. Place the carboy on the bottom shelf and attach the airline to the air pump system
2. Make sure the carboy is bubbling and then release the clamp on the exhaust
3. Attach a label onto the carboy with the algae species name, date, and source

**Don't forget to update the [Algae Tracker spreadsheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1UBeluqx4SQUdaInXP4of0siFTtXhhd97nKfENCC8lmE/edit?usp=sharing)!**
For the Carboy ID, the letter represents each new carboy made from a flask and the number is the re-bloom count.

## Re-blooming an Existing Carboy
Goals: 
- Give the algae more space and nutrients to continue to grow
- Keep the stock as sterile as possible to prevent bacteria contamination (which leads to algae die-off as a result of competition for resources)

Materials:
- 4L plastic bottle, autoclaved
- 5L glass bottle, autoclaved
- Respective caps with pre-drilled holes for airlines 
	- There should be one of each type in the lab
	- *Optional:* two normal caps if you want to shake them to dissolve salt
- 4L + 5L DI water
- 130g + 162.5g Instant Ocean (32.5g/L)
- 20mL + 25mL F/2 media, sterile and kept at 4ºC (5mL/1L *total volume*)
	- Store-bought F/2 media is used at different concentration (1mL/1L)
- 2 longer pieces of tubing, bleached

**IMPORTANT !! Whatever materials used in re-blooming must be clean and sterile !!** Additionally, the re-bloom bottles cannot be opened outside of the hood, to prevent bacteria contamination

Steps: 
Outside of hood—
1. Measure out Instant Ocean and add to plastic jug, immediately cover with lid
2. Fill with DI water, immediately replace lid
3. Swirl (or shake with cap on) until dissolved 
	- It is easier to swirl halfway and then continue to fill with DI water

In the hood—
1. Spray entire hood working surface with 70% ethanol
2. Uncap jug, put tubing inside, close the hood, and UV sterilize for 5 minutes
3. Add F/2 media and screw on lid
4. Attach hoses before removing from hood

In the oyster room—

![](https://lh7-rt.googleusercontent.com/docsz/AD_4nXcTr7orECP9Ia3jko4H_3-Pl52kBbjJcLIAQkyCRigSaeJwkCudExjh0OtqdaAYMdMcPiEXOE5A-aEjwoU-WSrF7_sdW9PdG4I6THdKj6sdrKJk6Zk06Q5nQ2HAFUErMz7HX2hJCg?key=JDzKcoJQwuC_JsCdwCkMPw)

Looking at the large carboy that needs to be re-bloomed, you have three lines coming off the top: airline, siphon, and exhaust. On the re-bloom bottle you have two: airline and siphon. If starting a carboy on the second shelf, you need to do the re-bloom on top of the shelf, but if the carboy is on the bottom shelf then the second shelf is fine because physics.

1. Close the exhaust line
2. Detach the airline from the carboy and attach it to one of the re-bloom bottles
	- This is coming directly from the air pump so the re-bloom bottle should start to bubble 
3. Connect the carboy siphon tube to the other tube coming out of the re-bloom bottle lid using a clean rubber connector
	- **The siphon tube needs to be closed** or else you will make a mess! 
4. Make sure the carboy exhaust is open, then open the siphon tube—there should be a flow of the contents from the re-bottle to the carboy
5. The contents of the re-bloom bottle should now be flowing into the carboy
	- Elevating the re-bloom bottle will help things move faster (gravity!)
6. When the re-bloom bottle is empty disconnect the tubes from the lid, starting with the airline
7. Repeat with the second re-bloom bottle
8. Once all of the salt water + F/2 media has been added to the carboy, rinse the tubing and rubber connectors before submerging in the bleach bath for 10 minutes 
	- If there isn't an existing bleach bath or it is more than two months old, refer to the [Bleach Baths](#Bleach%20Baths) section to make a new one
9. Close the siphon tube coming out of the carboy, and then reconnect the airline to the carboy
10. Rinse the re-bloom bottles with soap and water
11. After 10 minutes, remove the tubes and connectors from the bleach bath and submerge them in the DI bath for 10 minutes—let air dry next to the sink
12. All done :)

**Don't forget to update the [Algae Tracker spreadsheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1UBeluqx4SQUdaInXP4of0siFTtXhhd97nKfENCC8lmE/edit?usp=sharing)!**
For the Carboy ID, the letter represents each new carboy made from a flask and the number is the re-bloom count.

## Cleaning an Old Carboy
Goals:
- Make sure we always have sterile carboy materials on hand 
- Keep the oyster room looking fresh

Materials:
- Power drill, charged
- Rag-pole cleaning attachment

Steps: 
1. Take out the rubber stopper and detach all soft tubing and rubber connectors, NOT the three hard pieces of tubing
2. Thoroughly rinse all smaller components in the sink with soap and water, flushing the tubing as best as possible
3. Submerge all smaller components in the bleach bath, making sure there are no air pockets in the tubing, and let sit for 10 minutes
	- If there isn't an existing bleach bath or it is more than two months old, refer to the [Bleach Baths](#Bleach%20Baths) section to make a new one
4. While that is sitting, pour out the remaining algae from the carboy into a container and feed this off to the oysters (snack!)
5. Rinse out the carboy with soap and water 
6. Pre-wet the rag-pole cleaning attachment and then connect to the power drill to really get all of the algae bits off
	- **Important:** do not go full throttle on the power drill and do not use the rag-pole cleaning attachment when the carboy has water in it (too much drag)
7. Rinse out the carboy again and let it air dry
8. After 10 minutes, move the smaller components from the bleach bath into the DI bath and let sit for 5-10 minutes—let air dry next to the sink

The next day, put the dried smaller components away; we share the sink with other labs, so we don't want to monopolize the space. Once the carboy is dry, loosely cap tinfoil on the top and autoclave. 

# Flask Cultures
Keep more sterile liquid stocks of all algae species in case of carboy contamination or population crash. Should be swirled every day or stored on a shaker table to ensure oxygen exchange, and re-bloomed every week. There should only be one flask per species, unless planning to re-bloom a carboy in the next week. In that case, a second flask should be made prior to [Moving from Flasks to Carboy](#Moving%20from%20Flasks%20to%20Carboy).

**When handling the algae at this stage, work in the gray hood and spray things with 75% ethanol to keep things sterile. ==Only open one species of algae at a time when working in the hood to prevent cross-contamination!==**

## Re-bloom Flasks
Goals: 
- Keep algae at a happy growth phase
- Replenish nutrients

Materials:
- 500 mL flask, autoclaved
- 100mL DI water
- 3.25g Instant Ocean (32.5g/L)
- 1mL F/2 media, sterile and kept at 4ºC (5mL/1L *total volume*)
	- Store-bought F/2 media is used at different concentration (1mL/1L)
- 25mL glass serological pipette, autoclaved
- 100mL dense liquid algae culture 
- Foam stopper, autoclaved

> FOR 500 ML FLASKS DO 100 ML ALGAE AND 100 ML SALTWATER! DO NOT SPLIT TOO LOOSEY GOOSEY! -JS

Steps:
Outside of hood—
1. Record how much you plan to split in the [Algae Tracker spreadsheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1UBeluqx4SQUdaInXP4of0siFTtXhhd97nKfENCC8lmE/edit?usp=sharing) (flask re-blooms tab) and label flasks
	- Species, date, initials, re-bloom number
2. Measure out Instant Ocean and add to flask, immediately cover with tinfoil
3. Add DI water to flask, immediately replace tinfoil
4. Swirl flask until dissolved

In the hood—
1. Add F/2 media to flask and swirl
2. Pipette liquid algae culture into flask and swirl
3. Put in foam stopper and return flask to the shaker in 505

Any extra liquid algae culture not used in the re-bloom can be fed off to the oysters upstairs as a snack! 

## Moving from Flasks to Carboy
Goals:
- Grow up algae—let's get them dividing! 

Materials:
- 2L flask, autoclaved
- 1800mL DI water
- 58g Instant Ocean (32.5g/L)
- 10mL F/2 media, sterile and kept at 4ºC (5mL/1L *total volume*)
	- Store-bought F/2 media is used at different concentration (1mL/1L)
- 25mL glass serological pipette, autoclaved
- 200mL dense liquid algae culture
- Long piece of tubing

Steps:
Outside of hood—
1. Record how much you plan to grow up in the 2L flask in the [algae flask spreadsheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1UBeluqx4SQUdaInXP4of0siFTtXhhd97nKfENCC8lmE/edit?usp=sharing) (flask re-blooms tab) and label flasks
	- Species, date, initials, re-bloom number
2. Measure out Instant Ocean and add to flask, immediately cover with tinfoil
3. Add DI water to flask, immediately replace tinfoil
	- Periodically swirl flask to help dissolve salt

In the hood—
1. Add F/2 media to flask and swirl
2. Pour liquid algae culture into flask
3. Secure a piece of parafilm to the top of the flask

In the oyster room near the carboys and grow lights—
1. Use a clean (bleached) airline and connect it to the main air system
2. Peel back the parafilm and position the airline almost touching the bottom of the 2L flask
3. Secure the airline in place with the parafilm (can use a little piece of tape too) and make sure it is bubbling
	- Algae cultures do not need an airstone

Keep an eye on the flask to make sure 1) parafilm remains secure so there is no evaporation, 2)  airline stays in place and continues to bubble, 3) we have a sterile carboy prepared for when the 2L flask becomes sufficiently dense

# Slant Cultures
Long-term storage of algae species that don't require regular maintenance; can stay at room temperature for 6-12 months. We want to make sure that only algae (not bacteria) grows so first we plate the algae from liquid culture to try and isolate an algae colony. 

**Ensure all materials are autoclaved and work is done either over a bunsen burner or in the gray hood. These are our most sterile stocks!** 

## Agar Plates
Goals:
- To take algae from a flask or carboy and isolate colonies that are bacteria-free
- First necessary step to making slants! 

#### Making plates
Materials:
- 500mL flask
	- Size up if volume of DI water is >200mL (there is a liquid autoclave step)
- Graduated cylinder
- 140mL DI water
	- 20mL per plate: 20 * (6 plates + 1 extra) = 140mL
- 0.46g Instant Ocean (3.25g/L)
- 2.8g Bacto agar
	- 2% agar: 140mL * 0.02 = 2.8g
- 700uL F/2 media, sterile and kept at 4ºC (5mL/1L *total volume*)
	- Store-bought F/2 media is used at different concentration (1mL/1L)
- 6 glass plates, autoclaved
- 25mL glass serological pipette, autoclaved

**Note:** the exact amounts of water, salt, agar, and F/2 media depend on the number of plates to be made, so scale as needed. As a general rule of thumb, it is always good to make an extra plate (20mL) of master-mix. 

Steps:
Outside the hood—
1. Weigh out Instant Ocean and add to flask
2. Add DI water to flask and swirl until dissolved
3. Weigh out Bacto Agar and add to flask
4. Use a stir bar to help dissolve agar if necessary

In the hood—
1. Add F/2 media 
2. Cover flask loosely with tinfoil and autoclave for 20 minutes
	- **Make sure the autoclave is on the liquid (slow) setting**
3. Once moderately cool, pipette 20mL into each plate, leaving the covers off
	- ==Test adding antibiotics to the agar post-autoclave?==
	- Because the agar will start to set as it cools you want to do this when the flask is still relatively hot, so be careful! 
4. After all plates have been poured, make sure to fully eject any remaining media from the serological pipette so it doesn't get clogged when the agar sets
	- If agar does get stuck: fill a 500mL flask with ~200mL water and microwave for 2 minutes, then carefully (as the water will be very hot) submerge the pipette tip, wait 30 seconds, and then use the pipette gun to expel the re-melted agar
5. Once the agar is set and cooled (~45 minutes) replace each glass cover and wrap all edges with parafilm
	- Agar must be completely cool to prevent condensation 
6. Label the plates and store in 4ºC until ready to streak, or proceed with the protocol

Materials:
- Bunsen burner
- Flame loops
- Pre-poured agar plates
- 1mL aliquot of algae from liquid culture

On the bench—

2. Sterilize the flame loops by holding them in the flame until the metal gets red-hot; let them cool down completely before use

## Agar Slants
Goals: 
- Create a large agar surface area within a glass test tube to sustain algae long-term
- Act as "backup" stocks in case of liquid culture population crashes
	- Ideally have 2-3 per species

#### Making slants
Materials:
- 1000mL flask
	- Size up if volume of DI water is >400mL (there is a liquid autoclave step)
- Graduated cylinder
- 140mL DI water
	- 20mL per tube: 20 * (12 tubes + 1 extra) = 260mL
- 0.85g Instant Ocean (3.25g/L)
- 5.2g Bacto agar
	- 2% agar: 260mL * 0.02 = 5.2g
- 1.3mL F/2 media, sterile and kept at 4ºC (5mL/1L *total volume*)
	- Store-bought F/2 media is used at different concentration (1mL/1L)
- 12 large glass test tubes with covers, autoclaved
- 25mL glass serological pipette, autoclaved

**Note:** the exact amounts of water, salt, agar, and F/2 media depend on the number of plates to be made, so scale as needed. As a general rule of thumb, it is always good to make an extra tube (20mL) of master-mix. 

Steps:
Outside the hood—
1. Weigh out Instant Ocean and add to flask
2. Add DI water to flask and swirl until dissolved
3. Weigh out Bacto Agar and add to flask
4. Use a stir bar to help dissolve agar if necessary

In the hood—
1. Add F/2 media 
2. Cover flask loosely with tinfoil and autoclave for 20 minutes
	- **Make sure the autoclave is on the liquid (slow) setting**
3. Once moderately cool, pipette 20mL into each tube, leaving the caps off
	- Because the agar will start to set as it cools you want to do this when the flask is still relatively hot, so be careful! 
4. After all tubes are filled, make sure to fully eject any remaining media from the serological pipette so it doesn't get clogged when the agar sets
	- If agar does get stuck: fill a 500mL flask with ~200mL water and microwave for 2 minutes, then carefully (as the water will be very hot) submerge the pipette tip, wait 30 seconds, and then use the pipette gun to expel the re-melted agar
5. Rest the tubes at an almost horizontal angle (~10º) so the agar inside creates a nice gradual slope with lots of surface area:

6. Once the agar is set and cooled (~45 minutes) recap each tube and wrap all edges with a small piece of parafilm

#### Streaking slants
Materials:
- Unfiltered 1000uL pipette tips
- Pre-poured agar slants
- Agar plates with distinct, isolated algae colonies

Steps:
In the hood—
1. Using an unfiltered 1000uL pipette tip, lightly touch the end to a discrete algae colony from the plate
2. Streak the same pipette tip across the agar surface of the slant, working from the bottom up to the top
	- Apply enough pressure to gently etch the slant agar surface, as this will provide more surface area for the algae to grow
3. Parafilm the tubes and put them on the lab bench to encourage slow grow

Check after 1-2 weeks for signs of colorful algal growth! These can stay on the bench for 6-12 months—great job! 

## Moving from Slants to Flask
Goals:
- When a liquid flask crashes, we can go back to our slants to start fresh! 

Materials:
- 250mL flask, autoclaved
- 50mL DI water
- 1.63g Instant Ocean (3.25g/L)
- 250uL F/2 media, sterile and kept at 4ºC (5mL/1L *total volume*)
	- Store-bought F/2 media is used at different concentration (1mL/1L)
- Slant with visible algae colonies and no obvious bacteria growth
- Foam stopper, autoclaved

Steps:
Outside of hood—
1. Measure out Instant Ocean and add to flask, immediately cover with tinfoil
2. Add DI water to flask, immediately replace tinfoil
3. Swirl flask until dissolved

In the hood—
1. Add F/2 media to flask and swirl
2. Pipette 1mL of saltwater from the flask into the agar slant
3. "Rinse" the agar slant by pipetting up and down the same 1mL of saltwater until most of the algae becomes dislodged
4. Add the 1mL of saltwater + algae back into the flask
5. Put in foam stopper and return flask to the shaker in 505

Once this smaller flask is dense, plan to grow the algae up in a 500mL flask as described in the [Re-bloom Flasks](#Re-bloom%20Flasks) section.
