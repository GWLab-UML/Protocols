# Media

## Culturing Media
Based off of [Liu et al. 2023](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0031182023000768)
- No serum
- No antibiotics

Materials:
- 1L flask
- *Dry chemicals:*
	- 27g Instant Ocean, ~25ppt
	- 4.2g DME (4°C)
	- 5.4g Ham's F12 (4°C)
	- 0.15g L-glutamine 
	- 5.96g HEPES powder **
	- 0.65g sodium bicarbonate **
	- 0.7g glucose **
		- *0.1 g trehalose and galactose; replaced with more glucose
	- 0.015g glycine betaine (4°C)
		- *20mL fetal bovine serum* 
- 990mL DI water
- 10mL 100X lipid concentrate
	- *0.5mL phenol red; additional pH indicator, red is neutral and pink is basic
- Vacuum pump
- Filter funnel, base, and clamp - *sterile*
- 1L vacuum flask - *sterile*
- 0.22um filter paper
- 1L glass screw cap bottle - *sterile* 

Steps:
1. Weigh out all dry chemicals individually and add to 1L flask
	- Glycine betaine should be weighed on the more precise scale
2. Add 600mL DI water and use stir bar to dissolve fully, 30 minutes
3. In the BSC, add lipid concentrate and carefully pipette up and down to mix
4. Add remaining 390mL DI water
5. Check and record pH and salinity of solution
	- If pH is greater than 8, add HCl until pH is 7.2-7.4 (salmon color)
		*6/23/26 JF - this was about 9mL 10% HCl but be careful not to overshoot correction*
6. Back in the BSC, firmly insert the filter base into the filtration flask and set up the vacuum pump on the table outside of the BSC
7. Connect the vacuum pump tubing to the side arm of the flask and plug in the vacuum pump
8. Place an 0.22um filter onto the top of the filter base with tweezers
9. Carefully put the filter funnel on top of the filter base, sandwiching the filter paper, and use the clamp to secure
10. Add 200mL to the filter cup and switch the vacuum pump on
11. Continue to add solution to the filter cup until all 1L has been filtered
12. Slowly pour the filtered culturing media into a labeled, sterile 1L glass bottle
13. Store sterile culturing media at 4°C 
#### Notes
- Add 2% (v/v) antibiotics before in smaller batches before use
- Alternative culturing media DME:Ham's F-12 + 5% FBS

## Freezing media
Ratio is 10% DMSO, 50% FBS, and 40% culturing media (from José)

Materials:
- 250mL flask
- DMSO
- FBS (-20ºC)
- Culturing media (4ºC)
- Vacuum pump
- Filter funnel, base, and clamp - *sterile*
- 500mL vacuum flask - *sterile*
- 0.22um filter paper
- 100mL glass screw cap bottle - *sterile* 

Steps:
1. Calculate volumes needed of each reagent
	- ie. 50mL total = 5mL DMSO + 25mL FBS + 20mL culturing media
2. In the BSC add reagents into a flask and carefully pipette up and down to mix
3. Firmly insert the filter base into the filtration flask and set up the vacuum pump on the table outside of the BSC
4. Connect the vacuum pump tubing to the side arm of the flask and plug in the vacuum pump
5. Place an 0.22um filter onto the top of the filter base with tweezers
6. Carefully put the filter funnel on top of the filter base, sandwiching the filter paper, and use the clamp to secure
7. Add solution to the filter cup and switch the vacuum pump on
8. Once the whole solution has gone through, pour the filtered culturing media into a labeled, sterile glass bottle
9. Store sterile freezing media at 4°C
